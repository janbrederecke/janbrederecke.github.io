BACKGROUND

I have both a bachelor’s and a master’s degree in psychology. After my studies I initially worked as a researcher in psycho-oncology before transitioning to the domain of epidemiology. Here I use a mixture of classical statistical and modern machine learning methods to conduct lots of survival analyses in different cohort studies and build predictive models for the onset of cardiovascular disease. I have been using R since I was an undergraduate and still learn something R everyday! As a side-hustle I am currently finalizing my doctoral thesis.

R PACKAGES

When I find the time, I develop R packages that are designed to improve data analysis workflows in my current projects. Right now I work on:

  • prepareD - functions for data preparation, project templates, and documentation.
  • analyzeD - functions for automated linear, logistic, and Cox regressions across multiple predictors / outcomes.
  • visualizeD - functions for often used variants of ggplot2 visualizations.
  • predicteD - functions to help build Shiny apps based on predictive models that cannot be exporting including the original data (important e.g. in Cox-models).

Some of the packages are still in their early development stages but will be be updated regularly in the future. Additionally, I am a contributor to the climbeR package that is designed to visualize results of random forest models and will soon be available again on CRAN.
